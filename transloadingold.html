Definition

What Is
Transloading?

Transloading is the process of transferring cargo from one mode of transportation to another — whether container-to-truck, rail-to-truck, or vessel-to-warehouse — without routing freight to a final destination first.

It's a critical strategy for importers and exporters managing port congestion, cost optimization, and inland distribution. Done right, transloading cuts dwell time, reduces detention fees, and unlocks faster last-mile delivery.

Eastern Drayage has built its transloading platform around one principle: every transfer should add speed, not friction. Our facilities, equipment, and teams are purpose-built for this exact workflow.

01

02

03

Freight departs on Eastern's asset-based fleet to its final inland destination — tracked every mile.
What We Do

Our Transloading
Capabilities

Every operation is planned with cargo integrity, efficiency, and client accountability as primary drivers. We don't outsource execution — we own it.

Container-to-Truck Transfers
Direct off-loading from ISO containers onto domestic truckloads. Optimized for speed, minimizing terminal dwell and per-diem charges.
Rail-to-Truck Transfers
Intermodal transfers executed at Eastern-managed facilities near major rail ramps — including Kansas City, Chicago, and the NY/NJ metro.
Load Consolidation
Multiple inbound shipments merged into optimized outbound loads — reducing freight cost and improving cube utilization on long-haul routes.
Load De-Consolidation
Inbound consolidated shipments sorted and distributed to multiple consignees — streamlining final-mile delivery across regional markets.
Secure Staging & Warehousing
Freight staged in controlled indoor/outdoor facilities with real-time inventory accountability. Reducing dwell time is our default operating mode.
Cross-Docking
Rapid transfer of inbound freight directly to outbound transport with minimal storage time — ideal for time-sensitive, high-velocity shipments.
